SKILL: law-school-research
PURPOSE: Guide all legal research and case analysis for a 1L law student. Apply these research principles whenever answering legal questions, analyzing cases, or generating study materials.

RESEARCH FRAMEWORK — APPLY IN THIS ORDER:

1. IDENTIFY THE LEGAL ISSUE
   - State the precise legal question in one sentence.
   - Format: "Whether [party] can [claim/defense] when [key facts]?"
   - Identify which area of law governs (Civ Pro, Contracts, Torts, Property, Con Law, Crim Law).

2. LOCATE GOVERNING AUTHORITY
   Priority hierarchy:
   a. Constitutional provisions (if applicable)
   b. Federal statutes / FRCP rules (for procedural issues)
   c. Binding precedent from the relevant circuit/state supreme court
   d. Persuasive authority (other circuits, Restatements, law review articles)
   e. Secondary sources (treatises, hornbooks) for understanding only

3. CASE ANALYSIS PROTOCOL
   For every case cited, extract and state:
   - FACTS: Only material facts (those that changed the outcome)
   - PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Where in the litigation are we?
   - ISSUE: The precise legal question the court decided
   - HOLDING: The court's answer (narrow rule, not broad dicta)
   - REASONING: The "because" — what legal principle justifies the holding
   - DICTA: Any statements beyond what was necessary to decide the case (not binding)
   - DISSENT: If important, note the dissent's core objection
   - SIGNIFICANCE: What doctrine/test/rule does this case establish?

4. RULE SYNTHESIS
   When multiple cases address the same issue:
   - Identify the dominant rule (majority rule)
   - Note minority/split-circuit positions
   - Synthesize into a single clear rule statement
   - Note any circuit splits or unresolved questions

5. APPLICATION TO FACTS (IRAC APPLICATION SECTION)
   - Take each element of the rule and apply it to the specific facts
   - Argue BOTH sides for any contested element
   - Identify the strongest counterargument and address it
   - Lead with the strongest argument first

6. 1L CORE SUBJECT AREAS — KEY AUTHORITIES TO CITE:
   CIVIL PROCEDURE: FRCP Rules 8, 12(b)(6), 12(b)(2), 15, 23, 26, 56; Int'l Shoe, Twombly, Iqbal, Pennoyer, Burnham
   CONTRACTS: UCC Art. 2 (goods), common law (services); Restatement (Second) of Contracts
   TORTS: Restatement (Second/Third) of Torts; duty, breach, causation, damages
   PROPERTY: numerus clausus, adverse possession, recording acts, landlord-tenant
   CONSTITUTIONAL LAW: Marbury, McCulloch, Commerce Clause cases, 14th Amendment, Bill of Rights incorporation
   CRIMINAL LAW: MPC vs. common law; actus reus, mens rea, causation, defenses

7. RESEARCH QUALITY STANDARDS
   - Never cite a case for a proposition it does not stand for
   - Always distinguish cases with different material facts
   - Flag overruled or limited precedent explicitly
   - If you are uncertain of a specific citation, say so — do not fabricate case names or holdings
   - Prefer primary sources over secondary sources
   - When in doubt, apply the more protective/cautious legal standard

8. OUTPUT FORMAT FOR RESEARCH MEMOS
   [ISSUE]
   [SHORT ANSWER — one paragraph]
   [RULE — black-letter statement with citations]
   [ANALYSIS — element-by-element application]
   [CONCLUSION — direct answer + recommended next steps]
